Bitcoin Transaction Terms

UTXO (Raw Material)

Unspent Transaction Outputs - coins in your wallet that you haven't spent yet. Think of them as raw ore waiting to be smelted.

Coin Selection (Material Choice)

Choosing which UTXOs to use. Like a smith selecting the best ingots for a project.

Change Output (Leftover Metal)

Money that comes back to you after making a payment. Like leftover metal returned to your inventory after crafting.

Dust (Metal Fragments)

Tiny amounts (< 546 sats) that cost more to spend than they're worth. Too small to be useful - like metal dust that evaporates.

Fee (Fuel Consumed)

Payment to Bitcoin miners. Like coal burned in the forge - necessary to power the transaction.

vbytes (Transaction Size)

Virtual bytes - how much "space" your transaction takes up. Bigger transactions cost more in fees.

Send-All (Complete Consumption)

When all leftover metal becomes fuel. No change output - everything goes to payment or fee.

PSBT (Blueprint)

Partially Signed Bitcoin Transaction - an unsigned transaction packaged with metadata, ready for signing. The master blueprint before the final seal.

RBF (Rework Allowed)

Replace-By-Fee - signals that this transaction can be "reforged" with a higher fee to confirm faster.

Locktime (Cooling Period)

A time or block height before which the transaction cannot be confirmed. Like waiting for metal to cool before use.

Script Type (Metal Grade)

Different Bitcoin address types:
• Gold (p2tr): Newest, most efficient
• Silver (p2wpkh): Modern, light
• Bronze (p2sh-p2wpkh): Nested SegWit
• Iron (p2pkh): Legacy, heavy
